AI for your work life

Your inbox, your calendar, your career — AI tools can give you back hours every week. Not by replacing you, but by handling the busywork so you can focus on what actually matters.

Work Life

Career development

Your next job, promotion, or career pivot — AI can be the coach you didn't know you needed.

Whether you're polishing a resume, preparing for an interview, or figuring out your next career move, AI tools have become surprisingly good career advisors. They won't replace human mentors, but they're available at 2 AM when you're stress-editing your resume.

Resume and cover letter polish

Claude is remarkable for resume work. Paste in your current resume and a job description, and ask it to tailor your experience to the role. It'll rewrite bullet points to use stronger action verbs, highlight relevant skills, and catch awkward phrasing. It won't invent experience you don't have — but it'll make sure the experience you do have is presented at its best.

For cover letters, describe the job and what excites you about it. AI can draft a letter that sounds like you (not like a robot), hitting the key points the hiring manager wants to see. You edit and personalize it — but you're starting from a strong draft instead of a blank page.

Interview preparation

Tell Claude the role you're interviewing for, and ask for likely interview questions. Then practice answering them — the AI can play the interviewer, give you feedback on your answers, and suggest stronger ways to frame your experience. It's like having a mock interview partner available whenever you need one.

Networking and visibility

LinkedIn's AI features have quietly gotten very useful. The platform now suggests personalized connection messages, helps you optimize your profile for the roles you want, and even drafts post ideas to help you build professional visibility. The AI-powered "Top Voice" suggestions and article assistance make it easier to contribute to conversations in your field without spending hours writing.

Tools to try

Claude — Resume tailoring, cover letter drafting, interview prep, and career advice. Start with: "I'm applying for [role] at [company]. Here's my resume. Help me tailor it."

LinkedIn AI — Profile optimization, connection suggestions, and post drafting built right into the platform.

"I used Claude to prep for a senior manager interview. It asked me harder questions than the actual interviewers did."

Work Life

Inbox & calendar management

What if you could get back an entire workday every week?

That's not a hypothetical. Reclaim.ai users save an average of 7.6 hours per week — nearly a full workday. And they're not working less; they're spending their time on actual work instead of email ping-pong and scheduling gymnastics.

How AI tames your calendar

Smart scheduling. Tools like Reclaim.ai learn when you do your best focused work and automatically block those times on your calendar. They also find the optimal times for meetings by analyzing everyone's availability — no more "does Tuesday at 3 work? No? How about Thursday at 2? No?" email chains.

Focus time protection. Sunsama helps you plan each day intentionally. It pulls tasks from your various tools, asks you to commit to a realistic set for the day, and blocks time on your calendar for each one. If you're over-committed, it tells you — before the day starts.

Cross-platform coordination. Morgen syncs your personal and work calendars, consolidates scheduling links, and uses AI to suggest optimal meeting times across time zones. If you manage multiple calendars, this alone can save you an hour a day.

Email management

AI email tools can prioritize your inbox by urgency, draft responses, and even handle routine emails semi-autonomously. Instead of checking email 30 times a day, you check it twice — because the AI has already sorted what actually needs your attention.

Tools to try

Reclaim.ai — AI-powered calendar management. Automatically schedules focus time, one-on-ones, and habits. Integrates with Google Calendar. Their data shows an average of 7.6 hours saved per week per user.

Sunsama — Daily planning ritual that combines tasks from Asana, Trello, Gmail, and more into one calm interface. Helps you commit to a realistic day.

Morgen — Multi-calendar sync with AI scheduling. Perfect for people juggling personal and work calendars or working across time zones.

"I went from 12 hours of meetings per week to 6 — without missing anything important. Reclaim just found better slots for everything."

Work Life

Performance reviews

Never stare at a blank self-assessment again.

Performance review season is stressful for almost everyone. The biggest challenge isn't that your work wasn't good — it's that you forgot half of what you accomplished by the time the form is due. AI can help at every stage.

Track accomplishments throughout the year

The simplest habit: whenever something good happens at work, tell your AI. A quick note to Claude — "I led the Q3 product launch and we beat revenue targets by 15%" — builds a running log. Come review time, you have a complete record instead of trying to remember what you did nine months ago.

Some people keep a running note or a shared doc and ask AI to organize and categorize their accomplishments quarterly. Ten minutes every three months saves five hours of panic at year-end.

Draft self-assessments

Once you have your accomplishments collected, AI can help you write them up. Give Claude your list of wins and the format your company uses, and it'll draft a self-assessment that's clear, specific, and properly quantified. "Contributed to the project" becomes "Led cross-functional team of 6 to deliver the customer portal 2 weeks ahead of schedule, resulting in a 23% increase in user engagement."

Prepare talking points for the conversation

The review meeting itself can be nerve-wracking. AI can help you prepare talking points — not just about what you accomplished, but about what you want next. Ask it to help you articulate your career goals, identify areas for growth, and frame requests for advancement in a way that resonates with managers.

Process 360 feedback

If you receive 360 feedback, AI can help synthesize it. Paste in the anonymized feedback and ask for themes, patterns, and actionable takeaways. It's much easier to process constructive criticism when it's organized and summarized rather than coming at you in a raw document.

Tools to try

Claude — Your review season Swiss army knife. Logs accomplishments, drafts self-assessments, prepares talking points, and synthesizes feedback. Start with: "Help me write a self-assessment for my annual review. Here are my key accomplishments this year..."

The bigger picture

Performance reviews shouldn't be a dreaded annual event. With AI as a running journal and writing partner, they become a natural reflection on your growth — which is what they were always supposed to be.
